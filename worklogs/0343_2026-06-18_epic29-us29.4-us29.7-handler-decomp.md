### 1. US-29.4 — Extract `WorkspaceEnvHandler` (NEW file)

**New file:** `api/internal/handlers/workspace_env.go`
- `WorkspaceEnvHandler` struct with two dependencies: `WorkspaceEnvService` (caller-shaped interface, satisfied by `*secrets.SecretService`) and an optional logger.
- Constructor `NewWorkspaceEnvHandler(svc)` — fails at construction if svc is nil (US-29.8 principle).
- Three methods moved verbatim from `SecretsHandler`:
  - `SetWorkspaceEnv` (PUT /api/v1/workspaces/:id/env)
  - `GetWorkspaceEnv` (GET /api/v1/workspaces/:id/env)

**Removed from `secrets.go`:** the three methods + the now-unused `strings` import.

**Router wiring:** new optional `WorkspaceEnvHandler *handlers.WorkspaceEnvHandler` field on `RouterConfig`; the three env routes moved out of the `SecretsHandler` block into their own block. `workspaceGroup` is defined at package scope so the new block is reachable.

**app.go wiring:** `workspaceEnvHandler = handlers.NewWorkspaceEnvHandler(secretService)` constructed alongside `secretsHandler`; `SetLogger(log)`; passed to `RouterConfig.WorkspaceEnvHandler`.

## Key Decisions

1. **Caller-shaped interface, not full `SecretService`.** `WorkspaceEnvService` exposes only the 6 methods env-var management actually uses. This is the US-29.1 principle (interfaces should be caller-shaped) applied at the extraction boundary. `*secrets.SecretService` satisfies it via implicit implementation.

2. **Separate router block, not conditional on SecretsHandler.** The env routes are now in their own `if cfg.WorkspaceEnvHandler != nil` block. The two handlers can be constructed independently; in tests you can wire one without the other.

3. **Self-contained `doEnvRequest` helper.** The package-level `doRequest` (in `orgs_test.go`) uses `bytes.NewBufferString`; I chose `strings.NewReader` for the new tests. Rather than refactor the existing helper and risk breaking its callers, added a separate `doEnvRequest`. Trivial difference; both are test-only.

4. **Contract test uses recording backend, not per-route assertions.** A single backend that records auth presence across all routes is more maintainable than per-route `assertAuthSent` calls. Adding a new route to the proxy group just requires adding it to the routes table — the assertion is automatic.
